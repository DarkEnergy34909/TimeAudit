Privacy Policy

Effective Date: 25/07/2025

Your privacy is important to us. This Privacy Policy explains how TimeAudit collects, uses, and protects your personal data in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and Data Protection Act 2018.

1. Who we are

TimeAudit is a time-tracking web application based in the United Kingdom. If you have any questions about this policy or how your data is handled, you can contact us at support@timeaudit.net.

2. What data we collect

We collect the following types of personal data:

  • Email address: For user registration, account recovery and contact with users.
  • Password: For user authentication and account security.
  • Tracking/schedule data: Information about your activities, including time spent on tasks and scheduled events.

3. How we use your data

We use your personal data for the following purposes:

  • To provide and maintain our service, including user authentication and account management.
  • To improve our service by analysing usage patterns and user feedback.
  • To communicate with you about your account, including updates, security alerts, and support requests.

We do not use your data for advertising or sell it to third parties.

4. Legal basis for processing

We process your personal data based on the following legal grounds:

  • Consent: When you provide your email address and password to create an account.
  • Contractual necessity: To provide the service you have requested.
  • Legitimate interests: To improve our service and ensure its security.

You may withdraw your consent at any time by deleting your account or contacting us.

5. Data retention and deletion

We retain your personal data for as long as necessary to provide our service and fulfil the purposes outlined in this policy.

You can delete your account at any time, which will result in the removal of all your personal data from our systems permanently and immediately.

6. Data storage and security

We take the security of your personal data seriously and implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect it from unauthorised access, loss, or destruction.

  • All data is stored securely on our servers in the UK.
  • Access to personal data is restricted to authorised personnel only.
  • Passwords are hashed and not stored in plain text.

7. Your rights

Under the UK GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • The right to access your personal data.
  • The right to rectify inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • The right to erasure of your personal data.
  • The right to restrict processing of your personal data.
  • The right to data portability.
  • The right to object to processing.

To exercise these rights, contact us.

You have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) if you believe your rights have been violated.

8. Use of cookies and local storage

TimeAudit uses cookies and local storage to enhance your user experience, including:

  • Storing activity information to allow users to track activities without signing up.
  • Remembering your login information to keep you logged in.
  • Remembering your preferences and settings.

You can change your cookie settings at any time through your browser settings. However, this may affect the functionality of TimeAudit.

9. Third parties

We do not share your personal data with third parties except for cloud storage providers for data storage and processing purposes. These providers are bound by strict data protection agreements to ensure the security and confidentiality of your data.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated effective date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically for any changes.

The updated Privacy Policy will always be available here.
